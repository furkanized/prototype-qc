# Impeccable

## Design Context

### Users
Turkish Airlines internal product teams — Product Designers, UX Researchers, Business Analysts. They use QC Experience on desktop workstations to manage, launch and demo the Quick Check-in prototype to stakeholders. Context: office demos, design reviews, quick self-serve exploration.

### Brand Personality
Calm, premium, precise. Enterprise software that feels like Linear/Raycast/Vercel-grade craft, expressed through Turkish Airlines' identity (brand red `#d0021b` / `#C90C0F` on warm white, Geologica type, Takeoff UI tokens). Emotional goal: quiet confidence — "this airline sweats the details."

### Aesthetic Direction
- Light theme platform chrome around an untouched fixed-canvas prototype.
- Minimal surfaces, generous whitespace, motion only at high-impact moments (intro, launch transitions).
- The intro is the one sanctioned "art moment": Olafur Eliasson-like atmosphere — light as material, soft haze, a warm sun-halo in brand red/amber emerging from white, slow and volumetric, never flashy or neon.
- Anti-references: dark-mode-with-glow dashboards, cyan/purple AI gradients, particle confetti, bouncy easing.

### Design Principles
1. The prototype is sacred — the platform frames it, never competes with it.
2. One hero motion moment per flow; everything else is 150–300ms opacity/scale/translate.
3. THY red is the 10% accent; warm tinted neutrals carry the rest.
4. Every effect degrades gracefully (reduced motion, no-WebGL fallbacks).
5. Enterprise calm over spectacle — the wow should feel inevitable, not loud.
